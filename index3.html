EUROPEAN GROWN LINEN

European grown linen is a natural vegetable fibre grown in France, Belgium and the Netherlands.
Its production follows the European Flax® standard defined by the European Flax and Hemp Confederation (CELC), the federal authority of the world's linen agribusiness.
Its cultivation uses crop rotation, with few pesticides and fertilisers and no artificial irrigation*, no GM seeds and no defoliants. These techniques reduce the impact on soil, agricultural biodiversity and freshwater resources.



CERTIFICATIONS

The fibre production process is certified by European Flax® from the European Confederation of Linen and Hemp (CELC), a non-profit organisation that oversees all the production and transformation stages of linen and hemp.

Caring for your clothes is caring for the environment. Lower temperature washes and delicate spin cycles are gentler on garments and help to protect the colour, shape and structure of the fabric. Furthermore, they reduce the amount of energy used in care processes.



Machine wash at max. 30°C/86°F with short spin cycle

Do not use bleach

Iron at a maximum of 110 ° C/230°F

Dry clean with tetrachloroethylene

Do not tumble dry
